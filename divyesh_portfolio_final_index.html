Revenue Growth · Karagiri · Nov 2025 – Present

From a leaking P&L to 14% margins — in 4 months.

When I picked up Karagiri, the brand was doing ₹50L/month in revenue but haemorrhaging margin at every step — wrong SKUs taking up cash, RTO rates eating into fulfilment budgets, and low-intent COD orders inflating costs without converting to real revenue.

The fix wasn't one big move. It was three simultaneous levers pulled together: a hard SKU audit that delisted 30%+ of catalogue based on CM contribution, Gokwik risk scoring deployed at pincode level to filter high-RTO zip codes into prepaid-only flows, and a ₹99 COD commitment fee that cut out tyre-kickers without hurting genuine buyers. Pricing was rebuilt around a 66% margin floor with elasticity testing at the SKU level — which is where the AOV lift came from.

The chart below shows how CM2 moved month by month — not a one-time fix, but a compounding improvement as each lever took effect.

Operations · Karagiri · Logistics & Demand Quality

Cleaning the funnel from the bottom up.

High RTO and cancellation rates are silent margin killers in D2C — they show up as revenue on dashboards but drain cash through fulfilment, reverse logistics, and re-stocking costs. Karagiri had both problems in double digits.

RTO was tackled through Gokwik's risk engine — flagging high-risk pincodes and routing them into prepaid-only or PPCOD flows. Cancellations were driven by stock mismatches and vendor delays, so the fix was structural: a penalty framework for vendors missing SLAs, and real-time inventory delisting on Shopify when stock dropped below a threshold. Neither fix was glamorous — but together they freed up meaningful working capital.
